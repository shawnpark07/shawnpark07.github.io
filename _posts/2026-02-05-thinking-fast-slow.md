Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5)

A challenging but rewarding exploration of how we think.

## Summary

Nobel laureate Daniel Kahneman introduces the concept of two systems in our brain:
- **System 1**: Fast, intuitive, emotional
- **System 2**: Slow, deliberate, logical

Most of our decisions are made by System 1, which is efficient but prone to biases.

## Key Concepts

### Cognitive Biases That Stuck With Me

| Bias | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Anchoring** | Over-relying on the first piece of information |
| **Loss Aversion** | Losses feel ~2x worse than equivalent gains |
| **WYSIATI** | "What You See Is All There Is" - jumping to conclusions |

### The Planning Fallacy

We consistently underestimate how long tasks will take. Kahneman suggests using "reference class forecasting"—look at how long similar projects took others.

## Practical Applications

- When making important decisions, slow down and engage System 2
- Be aware of anchoring in negotiations
- Add buffer time to all estimates

## The Downside

It's dense. Some chapters feel repetitive. But the insights are worth the effort.
