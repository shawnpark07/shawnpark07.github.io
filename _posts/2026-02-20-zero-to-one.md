Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5)

Essential reading for anyone thinking about startups or innovation.

## Summary

Peter Thiel argues that true innovation means going from "zero to one"—creating something entirely new—rather than "one to n"—copying what works.

## Core Ideas

### Competition is for Losers

Counterintuitively, Thiel argues that competition destroys profits. The best businesses are monopolies that create unique value.

### The Power Law

A small number of investments/decisions will generate the majority of returns. Focus on finding those asymmetric opportunities.

### Secrets

Every great business is built on a secret—something important that most people don't know or don't agree with.

## Contrarian Questions

The book's most famous question:

> "What important truth do very few people agree with you on?"

This question forces you to think beyond conventional wisdom.

## Why It Resonated

As someone who worked at TADA/SOCAR, I saw firsthand how creating something new (ride-sharing in Korea) differs from competing in established markets.

## Who Should Read This

Founders, investors, or anyone who wants to understand what makes transformative companies different.
