One of those rare books that actually changed my behavior.

## Summary

James Clear argues that small, incremental changes (1% improvements) compound over time into remarkable results. The book provides a practical framework for building good habits and breaking bad ones.

## Key Takeaways

### The Four Laws of Behavior Change

1. **Make it Obvious** - Design your environment for success
2. **Make it Attractive** - Bundle habits with things you enjoy
3. **Make it Easy** - Reduce friction for good habits
4. **Make it Satisfying** - Create immediate rewards

### Favorite Quotes

> "You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems."

> "Every action is a vote for the type of person you wish to become."

## How I Applied It

I used the "habit stacking" technique to build a reading habit: After I pour my morning coffee (existing habit), I will read for 10 minutes (new habit).

## Who Should Read This

Anyone who has struggled to make changes stick. It's practical, evidence-based, and immediately actionable.
